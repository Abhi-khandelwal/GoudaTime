Why did we make this?

Solving a problem

Part of our reasoning for creating a service for finding restaurants was to solve an age old problem: agreeing on dinner. What we mean is that every couple, group of friends, or family has had the argument about where to eat tonight.

We set out to solve this issue by creating a service that allows people to join a group and only be matched with a restaurant if eevryone agrees, thus allowing unity and no argument.

Ease of use

Everyone knows how Tinder works and the simple swipe left or swipe right mechanism. We tried to replicate this simplicity and allow users to apply it to an every day problem such as dinner.

It is not limited to dinner, but is also applicable to all meals. By allowing users to just log in and form their group and immediately go to looking for food, they can decide on dinner while they're walking back from class or while on the train home from work.

Fun

Obviously, one of the main reasons we set out to finish this projects was to have fun. This project started as an idea from a hackathon and has bloomed into a full blown service for the average person to take advantage. We want everyone to have fun.

This is a free, open source project written by the lovely developers mentioned above and welcomes outsiders for contributions or suggestions. Please just refer to our GitHub page.
